Who we are

SPEAK Up BC was founded by high school students in Surrey, BC who recognized a critical gap: existing systems for reporting racism in schools had no accountability mechanism. Reports were made and forgotten. Schools faced no consequences for inaction. Students had no way to know whether anything was being done. We built this platform to change that.

Our values
Transparency
Every report has a trackable outcome. Every year, we publish what happened — not just promises.
Accountability
Schools that fail to act face escalating consequences. Inaction is documented publicly.
Confidentiality
Student identities are protected by design. We never share information unless safety requires it.
Independence
We are funded by grants and community contributions — never by the institutions we hold accountable.
Annual summary reports
Every year SPEAK Up BC publishes a public accountability report — anonymized aggregate data so communities can see whether schools are improving.
All reports are cross-reviewed by SPEAK Up BC staff, partner school districts, and an independent equity committee. Individual cases are never disclosed.

Why we publish this
Transparency is accountability. When schools know their response rates are publicly visible, they are more motivated to act. When students and families can see the data, they know whether the system is working. SPEAK Up BC believes sunlight is the most powerful tool for systemic change.
